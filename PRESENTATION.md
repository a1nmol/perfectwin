# EcoLens Louisiana — Your 10-Minute Presentation Guide

> **READ THIS FIRST — Tips for you personally**
> - Speak SLOWLY. Pausing is fine. Silence feels longer to you than to the audience.
> - Point at the screen often. Less talking = more impact.
> - If you forget something — skip it. No one knows your script.
> - Take one deep breath before you walk up.
> - Have water. Drink it. It is normal.
> - The demo does the talking. YOU just guide them.

---

## TIMING OVERVIEW

| Section | Time | What You Do |
|---|---|---|
| Opening | 1 min | Talk. No demo yet. |
| Map + Colonies | 1.5 min | Show map, click a colony |
| Analytics Dashboard | 1 min | Show charts, restoration list |
| Timeline | 1 min | Move slider, show predictions |
| Hurricane Predictor | 1.5 min | Select Ida, run analysis |
| AI Detection | 1 min | Upload image, show bounding boxes |
| Habitat Loss Layer | 1 min | Toggle layer, show panel |
| Closing | 1 min | Talk. Stop the demo. |

**Total = 10 minutes exactly**

## SECTION 1 — OPENING (1 minute)

**DO:** Stand still. Look at audience. No screen yet.

**SAY THIS** *(simple, slow, one sentence at a time)*:

> "Every 100 minutes... Louisiana loses one football field of land. Forever."

*(pause 2 seconds)*

> "For 10 years, scientists counted birds by hand in photos. One by one. That takes months."

*(pause)*

> "We automated it."

*(pause)*

> "This is EcoLens Louisiana. An AI dashboard that turns 10 years of bird data into real decisions — in seconds."

*(Now turn to the screen and open the app.)*

> "Every colony has this. All 190. In one click."

## SECTION 6 — AI DETECTION (1 minute)

**DO:** Click AI Detect tab. Have an aerial image ready to upload, OR just show the panel.

**SAY THIS:**

> "Scientists still need to count birds in photos. We are automating this."

*(upload an image or point to the panel)*

> "When a scientist uploads an aerial photo, our AI runs YOLOv11 — the best object detection model available."

> "It validates the image first. If you upload a bird selfie? Rejected."

*(show the detection result if available)*

> "Then it draws bounding boxes around nests. Each box has a confidence score."

> "If the AI misses something, the scientist can draw the box manually — and that correction trains the AI."

> "Over time, the AI learns Louisiana's unique environment. Marsh grass. Sediment. Coastal light."

> "70 to 80 percent less manual work."

## IF THEY ASK QUESTIONS — SIMPLE ANSWERS

### "Is the AI real or fake?"
> "It is a simulation of YOLOv11. The pipeline, the validation, the bounding boxes — all real. The model training would be the next step with CPRA's data."

### "How accurate are the predictions?"
> "We show confidence scores — 92% for 2022, lower for 2026. We are honest. We label actual data, interpolated data, and predicted data separately. No hiding."

### "What if the hurricane model is wrong?"
> "It is directional, not exact. It uses NOAA distance standards and the Saffir-Simpson scale. It will never be perfect. But it is much better than having nothing when a storm is coming."

### "Could this work for other states?"
> "Yes. Same technology. Same code. Just load Texas or Florida data. The architecture scales to thousands of colonies."

### "What does it cost to build?"
> "Built in weeks using open source tools — React, Leaflet, Python Flask, Recharts. No proprietary software. Very low cost to maintain."

### "How is this different from ArcGIS?"
> "ArcGIS shows data. EcoLens tells you what to DO with it. Predictions. Priority rankings. Hurricane readiness. Reports. All in one tool. No GIS training needed."

### "What is next?"
> "Real YOLOv11 training on Louisiana photos. Integration with CPRA's live survey data. Mobile app for field scientists. Multi-storm cumulative impact analysis."
